Commercial drain cleaning for kitchens, restrooms, and back of house

Floor drains, storm drains, and catch basins keep commercial spaces operating. Cleaning them on a schedule keeps surprise backups out of the day.

Overview

What this category covers

Commercial drain cleaning is different from residential because the volumes are bigger, the schedules are tighter, and the cost of downtime is higher. The fix is a planned cadence — not a reactive call when the floor drain finally overflows during a Friday rush.

Honest Drain cleans the lines, documents the work, and gives operators a clear picture of how the drain system is holding up over time so capital decisions can be planned, not surprised.

Drain Cleaning options

Each sub-service has its own write-up that explains the work, the signs that point at it, and the honest steps Honest Drain takes during the visit.

Floor Drain Cleaning

Commercial floor drains take everything: grease, food waste, mop water, and detergent. Cleaning them on a cadence prevents the slow drain that closes the kitchen.

Storm Drain Cleaning

Storm drains overflow when they are full of leaves, sand, and trash. Cleaning before the rainy season is cheaper than parking-lot flooding.

Catch Basin Cleaning

Catch basins capture grit and debris before it enters the storm system. They have to be vacuumed periodically or they stop catching anything at all.
